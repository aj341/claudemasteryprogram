Example 1Getting Claude to tell you where it's reliable — and where it isn't
Financial adviser · Testing Claude's self-awareness about limitations
I'm a financial adviser in Australia. Before I start using Claude regularly for work, I need an honest answer about your limitations. Tell me: 1. What your knowledge cutoff date is 2. What kinds of financial information I should never rely on you for 3. What kinds of information you ARE reliable for in my day-to-day advisory work Be direct and don't soften the limitations. I'd rather know upfront than find out mid-client-meeting.
Why it works: It establishes who's asking and why, invites honesty explicitly, and asks numbered questions — which gets a structured, useful answer. Asking for both reliable and unreliable areas produces a balanced response rather than just reassurance.
