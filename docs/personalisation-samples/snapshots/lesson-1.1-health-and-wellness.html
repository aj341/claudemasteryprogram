Example 3Getting calibrated advice on risk for a regulated environment
GP clinic manager · Asking Claude to articulate risks in your context
I manage a GP clinic in regional Queensland. I want to use Claude for administrative work only: patient communication templates, internal policy documents, and staff rostering language. Before I start, tell me: - What could go wrong if I'm not careful - Which kinds of decisions I should never delegate to Claude without human review - Any specific considerations for a regulated healthcare setting I'm not planning to use Claude for clinical decisions — just admin. But I still want to know the boundaries.
Why it works: Asking Claude to articulate risks in a specific professional context gets genuinely useful, calibrated advice. The framing ("I'm not planning to use it clinically, but...") helps Claude give a proportionate answer rather than a blanket warning.
